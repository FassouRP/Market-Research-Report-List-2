<p><p>The Bakery Enzymes market consists of various types of enzymes, including amylase, glucose oxidase, lipase, lipoxygenase, xylanase, protease, and asparaginase. Amylase helps break down starch molecules into smaller sugar units, while glucose oxidase converts glucose into gluconic acid, enhancing dough properties. Lipase improves the texture and flavor of bakery products. Lipoxygenase improves the dough fermentation process. Xylanase helps degrade hemicellulose, leading to increased dough extensibility. Protease improves dough elasticity and bread quality. Lastly, asparaginase helps reduce the formation of acrylamide in baked goods, a potential carcinogen.</p></p>
