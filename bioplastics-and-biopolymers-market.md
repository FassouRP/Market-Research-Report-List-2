<p><p>Bioplastics and biopolymers are sustainable alternatives to traditional plastics. Starch blends are bioplastics made by blending starch with other polymers, making them biodegradable. PLA (Polylactic Acid) is a biopolymer derived from renewable resources like cornstarch or sugarcane, offering biodegradability and compostability. Bio-PE (Bio-Polyethylene) is a bioplastic created from sugarcane ethanol and has similar properties to traditional polyethylene. PBAT (Polybutylene Adipate Terephthalate) is a biodegradable polyester used mainly in packaging. Bio-PET (Bio-Polyethylene Terephthalate) is a polyester made with renewable sources, which has lower carbon emissions. "Others" refer to various bioplastics and biopolymers made from different sources and used for different applications.</p></p>
